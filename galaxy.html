Overview

This project is based on Percolation Model of Galactic Structure by Philip E. Seiden and Lawrence S. Schulman. The paper presents a percolation-based model for simulating spiral galaxies using a stochastic cellular automaton.

The paper describes two models: a simple percolation model and a gas-regulated model. Our work focused on recreating both models, understanding the assumptions needed to reproduce their behavior, and testing how well local stochastic rules can produce large-scale spiral structure.

Presentation

We presented this project at the Virginia Academy of Science 2026 Annual Meeting and won Best Poster in Astronomy, Math and Physics.

Our Contributions

Open-source recreation of both models

We created an open-source recreation of both the simple model and the gas-regulated model. To our knowledge, while there are other recreations of parts of this work, there was not an open-source recreation of both models together.

This matters because the percolation process is stochastic, and the original paper does not provide every implementation detail needed to directly reproduce the results. We implemented the models and recreated many of the paper's results, which supports the accuracy of our implementation.

Connection to early galaxy formation

Using the simple model, we took inspiration from the Firefly Sparkle, an observed galaxy in the process of formation whose stellar mass is concentrated in about ten star clusters.

We tested whether this kind of clustered beginning could be consistent with percolation-driven galaxy formation. We found that even starting with as few as 1–10 initial star-forming clusters can lead to a nonzero creation rate of natural-looking spiral galaxies in the simple model.
