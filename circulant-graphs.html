Overview

This project studies independent sets in families of circulant graphs that arise from the frequency assignment problem on square and cellular grids. Our goal was to find the independence number of these graphs and use that information to bound frequency separation in optimal channel assignments.

Main Results

Square-grid family

For the circulant graph family S k arising from square grids, we determined the exact independence number α ( S k ) = k 2 .

Cellular-grid family

For the circulant graph family H k arising from cellular grids, we found an upper bound on the independence number α ( H k ) ≤ k 2 + k 2 .

Generalized family

Motivated by the shared structure in the square-grid and cellular-grid families, we defined a broader infinite family of circulant graphs G ( r , k ) containing both grid-derived families as special cases.

For this generalized family, we established general upper and lower bounds on the independence number. We also determined the independence number exactly when r = 4 and obtained an improved upper bound when r = 5 .
